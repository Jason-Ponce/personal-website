Problem Statement
For our initial project, we were challenged to explore the creativity of our environment by limiting ourselves to a letter in the alphabet. My choice letter was the letter H.
Images 1-10
Reflection

The initial work on the pictures brought a lot of problems at first. For the first couple of days that the project ran for. I had no idea how to begin or even what letter to choose. As you can see, the very first photo I took spoke to me. It was the park benches at my local park. Looking at a side profile, I saw multiple H's and from there I kept seeing H's everywhere I walked. The next 9 photos came to me all in the same day. It wasn't a problem at this point. But knowing that the next part of the project required that I take another 40 shots got me thinking that I needed to expand my search on where I looked.

Bottom 3 From Images 11-50
Learning from Mistakes

The pictures I choose as the bottom 3 were in my opinion, unoriginal and not really worth being part of the project as a whole. The problem with these photos is that they just are. There is nothing special about them and they would be obvious choices that one would take when limited in time.

How To Improve

I touched base with a possible solution in my previous post. Some of the images that I personally don't feel like would be worthy in my overall projects. Were the photos that were taken when the deadline was nearing. Whether it was due to my time mismanagement with other commitments or miscounting the total amount of photos I had. Given more time on either my part or from the project constraints. I feel like I could've explored more interesting idea.

For the photos I was proud of, they came around from my walks with my dog. During these times, I had no other worry going on in my life. It was just a walk through a park, a trail, around my neighbourhood without a care in the world. Except for my dog, who kept pulling me, making the photo taking process a challenge in itself.

Generally though, my best path towards taking photos and for overcoming designers block. Was spending time with my dog. I ended up just walking around and taking photos of whatever came by. Taking my time to slow down and really appreciate my environment. I guess through this exploration of my surroundings, it allowed me to see more than I would've had I just walked around without aim.

Highlights From Images 51-100
Couble Down

For the final leg of the project, the requirement was to take 50 photos in a shorter time frame. I did feel the crunch as I was lacking 10 or so photos towards the night of the last day. It wasn't until the morning of the due date that I finished with taking my photos. Which didn't give me much time to edit and finalize the images for show and tell. My best practice in getting more photos that I was proud of was just to go explore. I wasn't a big fan of taking objects and "putting" them together and call it a letter. I personally feel like its not as creative as going out and finding what the environment itself has to offer.

Conclusion
My Favorite Image

In a whole, I really enjoyed my first 10 pictures the most. Out of the first 10 I really enjoyed taking the one shown above. Taking the photo required some thought from my ending. Its not something apparent unless if you took the time to stop and focus on the different perspectives.

Overall Experience

Towards the end of the project, I was feeling the stress of coming up with the 100 photos that were required. But I enjoyed spending time with my dog and figuring out new perspectives that can come to me given the time invested if i just looked long enough. Also I feel like without my dog, many of my photos wouldn't have included many outdoor photos. For me this project was the perfect excuse to take my dog out and explore.

All in all I would say that I was successful in achieving the goals I had for myself. Given a whole semester, I would've loved to expand on the idea and really double down on the constraints of the project. Understandably though this class is about graphic design and not about taking photos of random letters in the wild. But as far as the presentation of the final pdf poster. I would've given it more thought about the layout of the images in the overall structure of the images as a whole. Overall I did enjoy the project and learned a lot.
