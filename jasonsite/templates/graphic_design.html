Travel Poster

  • Cover Page

    The project was intended to showcase a famous figure in history. The cover was done in representation of the two ruler Emperor justinian and Empress theodora. Important to note that the correct name of Byzantium was Eastern Roman Empire as they still considered themselves Roman.

  • Introduction

    The pamphlet consisted of 8 pages, a front, back, and 6 content pages. One of the lessons learned from designing a pamphlet is the design and layout can be applied to web page creation.

  • Page 2

    Even simple pen and paper planning goes a long way in figuring out how the basic design of any project. An overarching style can be created and applied to each page that unifies the whole experience.

  • Page 3

    This pamphlet was created on InDesign. If you were in a rush a small 8 page pamphlet could easily be done without working knowing any of the advanced features InDesign has to offer. Thankfully learning how to use the master pages and applying styles makes the whole project a dynamic work instead of a static word page.

  • Page 4

    Learning how to create documents, such as pamphlets is a perfect way to begin learning web designing. Much like how one could read the newspaper and learn techniques on how to design as website.

  • Page 5

    Back to creating a pamphlet for the Eastern Roman Empire. Careful thought was needed to select the correct font, font color, the background color, images and design. For example, black text would've worked on the text blocks of the pamphlet. But it's not easy on the eyes.

  • Page 6

    The map used here is also a free to use image. The last thing that a product needs is for legal action for using material that wasn't licensed. Which is another important step to take when developing a website. Don't do evil.

  • Back Page

    And this is the last page. The front and back page was entirely designed by me and i'm just a big fan of it, minus the eagle symbol which is under creative commons and the font.
